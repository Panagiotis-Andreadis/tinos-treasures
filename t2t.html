The story

Why T2T exists

I’ve always believed that the development model of the Greek islands — based almost entirely on tourism and real estate — is not truly sustainable. Tourism brings income, yes. But it does not create depth. It does not create character. It does not create the conditions for a vibrant, permanent population that keeps an island authentic and alive all year long.

For islands to remain places and not just products, they need something deeper: infrastructure that offers quality of life, safety and long-term stability. At the heart of this lie two essential pillars: healthcare and education. We decided to start with healthcare.

I am a Naval Architect and Mechanical Engineer working in the private sector, but with a deep passion for giving back, for hospitality culture, and for cooking. One of my strange but very real dreams has always been to work for a while in a small souvlaki shop: wrapping pitas, sweating, laughing with customers, living the authenticity of everyday food.

From an idea to a social act

I love hard work. I love being around people. And I feel a genuine need to contribute.

This is how the idea of a canteen was born. Not as a business move, but as a social act.

A one–month pop-up canteen that:

  • works with local ingredients and local people,
  • creates a shared sunset experience in Tinos, and
  • donates 100% of net profits to the Tinos Health Centre.
How it works

A sunset canteen with a purpose

“Every sandwich and every cocktail helps fund better healthcare in Tinos.”

T2T is a non-profit, sunset pop-up canteen operating from 17:00 to 22:00 in Plakiani, Tinos, from 20 July to 20 August 2026.

We keep the menu intentionally simple and local:

  • 3 signature sandwiches with local bread, smoked meats, capers, artichoke, sun-dried tomatoes, and island-inspired sauces like Kariki cream, Petro sauce and tomato jam.
  • 4 drinks & cocktails including a margarita with local citrus, a tsipouro-based cocktail, a mastiha spritz and a herbal lemonade.

Behind the counter there is hard work, sweat, laughter and conversation. In front of it, there is a view of the Aegean sunset — and the knowledge that what you’re enjoying is directly helping the island.

What makes T2T different?
  • Non-profit: all net earnings are donated to the Tinos Health Centre.
  • Local-first: we source as locally as possible and give back locally.
  • Transparent: audited results, clearly communicated impact.
  • Cultural: Greek alternative/90s music, Cycladic minimal design, authentic hospitality.

This is a project about food, yes. But even more than that, it is about place, people and future.

Impact

From Tinos → To Tinos

T2T is designed as a circular island project: we source from Tinos, we operate in Tinos, and we give back to Tinos.

The goal is to donate at least €10,000 net to the Tinos Health Centre after one month of operation. Beyond this number, the true impact is about:

  • strengthening healthcare as a basic condition for year-round life on the island,
  • raising awareness that tourism alone is not enough, and
  • showing that small, focused projects can create real change.

A stronger health system makes it easier for families and young people to choose Tinos not just for a week in August — but as a place to live.

Why healthcare?

For an island to have a future, it needs more than visitors. It needs permanent residents who feel safe, supported and able to raise families there.

Healthcare is a non-negotiable foundation. By supporting the Tinos Health Centre, we are investing in:

  • the dignity and safety of the local community,
  • the resilience of the island, and
  • a healthier, more sustainable model of tourism.
